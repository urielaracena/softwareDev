Kyoto & Cultural Triangle

Experience traditional Japan through ancient temples, shrines, and cultural treasures

Welcome to Kyoto

Once the imperial capital of Japan for over a thousand years, Kyoto embodies the heart and soul of traditional Japanese culture. With its 1,600 Buddhist temples, 400 Shinto shrines, picturesque gardens, and historic districts, Kyoto offers visitors an immersive journey into Japan's rich heritage and timeless beauty.

Purchase a Kyoto Bus Pass for unlimited travel on city buses, which are the primary mode of public transport. Consider renting bicycles to explore the flat city center. For visits to nearby Nara and Osaka, the JR line is convenient and covered by the Japan Rail Pass.

üèØ Temple Visiting

Many popular temples charge entrance fees (typically 300-600 yen). The best times to visit are early morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds. Wear comfortable slip-on shoes as you'll be removing them frequently. Some temple areas restrict photography, so always check for signs.

üëò Cultural Experiences

Book traditional experiences like tea ceremonies, kimono rentals, and cooking classes in advance, especially during cherry blossom and autumn foliage seasons. Gion district offers opportunities to glimpse geisha (known locally as geiko) during evening hours.

üçµ Dining

Try Kyoto's refined cuisine known as "Kyo-ryori," featuring delicate flavors and seasonal ingredients. Vegetarians can enjoy shojin ryori (Buddhist temple cuisine) at various establishments. For budget options, the food floor of department stores offers excellent quality and variety.

üìÖ Seasonal Considerations

Cherry blossom season (late March to early April) and autumn foliage (November) are breathtakingly beautiful but extremely crowded. Book accommodations months in advance for these periods. Consider visiting during May or October for pleasant weather with fewer tourists.

üö∂ Day Trips

Allocate time for day trips to nearby Nara (to see the famous deer park and Todai-ji Temple), Osaka (for modern urban experiences and street food), and Arashiyama (for bamboo groves and monkey park). Each is easily accessible by train from Kyoto Station.

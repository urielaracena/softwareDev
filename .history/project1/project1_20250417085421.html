Top Tourist Experiences in Japan

Visiting an onsen (hot spring bath) is a must-do for travelers looking to unwind in Japan's beautiful natural settings. Found in scenic areas like Hakone, Beppu, and Kusatsu, onsens offer soothing mineral-rich waters. Many ryokan offer private baths. Be sure to shower thoroughly before entering, and keep towels and hair out of the water.

If you're visiting in spring, don't miss hanami—Japan's iconic cherry blossom viewing. Visit Ueno Park in Tokyo or Maruyama Park in Kyoto. Bring snacks or buy a hanami bento box, and enjoy the blossoms during the day or at night with light-up displays.

Animal cafés are a unique Japan experience. Enjoy drinks while petting cats, owls, hedgehogs, or Shiba Inus. They're especially popular in Tokyo areas like Harajuku and Shibuya. There are entry fees, time limits, and gentle rules to protect the animals.

Touring a Japanese castle is a great way to dive into samurai history. Visit Himeji, Matsumoto, or Osaka Castle. These castles offer museum exhibits, historical interiors, and scenic views from the top floors.

Join a tea ceremony to experience Japanese aesthetics and hospitality. You can take part in Kyoto, Tokyo, and many cultural centers. Learn the graceful steps of making matcha tea and enjoy traditional sweets too.

Visit iconic temples like Fushimi Inari Shrine in Kyoto or Meiji Shrine in Tokyo. Purify your hands, walk the paths, and try an omikuji (fortune slip). Many shrines are surrounded by gardens and local food stalls for a full experience.

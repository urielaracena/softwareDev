Beautiful Japanese Flora

Japanese Maple Sakura (Cherry Blossom) Japanese Peony Japanese Iris Nandina Japanese Camellia

Japanese Maple

Renowned for spectacular foliage displays with delicate, palmate leaves that transform from vibrant spring colors to fiery autumn shades. Hundreds of cultivars offer variations in leaf shape, size, and color, creating dappled shade and airy lightness in garden settings.

Sakura (Cherry Blossom)

Japan's most iconic flower produces delicate five-petaled blossoms ranging from white to pink. Blooms appear in dramatic profusion along branches before leaves emerge, creating ethereal clouds of color that last just 1-2 weeks, embodying the Japanese aesthetic principle of mono no aware.

Japanese Peony

Tree peonies produce spectacular dinner-plate sized blooms measuring 8-12 inches across with silky, ruffled petals in colors ranging from pure white to nearly black purple. These woody shrubs maintain their structure year-round with deeply lobed, blue-green leaves that turn bronze-purple in fall.

Japanese Iris

Produces elegant flowers with six flattened petals that spread horizontally. These striking 4-6 inch blooms appear in early summer in colors from white to purple, often with contrasting veining or edges. The plants form clumps of tall, blade-like foliage reaching 2-4 feet high.

Nandina

Offers year-round interest with constantly changing foliage colors. Its pinnately compound leaves emerge coppery-pink before turning blue-green in summer and brilliant red-orange in autumn. In winter, bright red berries persist, creating striking contrast against the colorful foliage.

Japanese Camellia

Brings exceptional floral beauty during winter with large, perfectly formed flowers in shades from white to deep red. The blooms can be simple singles with golden stamens or complex peony forms, set against glossy dark green foliage that provides excellent garden structure year-round.

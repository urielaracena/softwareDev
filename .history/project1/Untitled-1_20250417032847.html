Popular Japanese Dishes

Sushi Ramen Unagi Yakitori Okonomiyaki Katsu Curry

Sushi

A dish of vinegared rice combined with fresh raw fish, seafood, or vegetables. The rice is seasoned with rice vinegar, sugar, and salt, then topped or filled with ingredients like tuna, salmon, yellowtail, shrimp, or cucumber.

Ramen

A noodle soup dish featuring wheat noodles served in a meat-based broth, flavored with soy sauce or miso, and topped with ingredients like sliced pork (chashu), dried seaweed (nori), green onions, and soft-boiled eggs.

Unagi

A traditional delicacy of freshwater eel grilled with a sweet soy-based sauce and typically served over rice.

Yakitori

Skewered chicken pieces grilled over charcoal. Different parts of the chicken are used including breast, thigh, skin, liver, heart, and cartilage, each offering distinct textures and flavors.

Okonomiyaki

A savory pancake containing a variety of ingredients in a wheat-flour-based batter. The batter includes grated nagaimo (mountain yam), dashi, eggs, and shredded cabbage.

Katsu Curry

A dish combining crispy breaded cutlet with Japanese curry sauce, served over rice.

Japanese Regional Cuisine

Hokkaido Tohoku Kanto Kansai Kyushu Okinawa

Hokkaido

Known for fresh seafood, dairy products, and soup curry. Signature dishes include Genghis Khan (grilled lamb), fresh uni (sea urchin), and Sapporo ramen with miso-based broth.

Tohoku

Features hearty dishes designed for cold winters. Specialties include kiritanpo (pounded rice on sticks), hittsumi (flat dumplings in soup), and jappa-jiru (fish head soup).

Kanto

Home to Tokyo's diverse food scene. Known for monjayaki (runny savory pancake), chanko nabe (sumo wrestler hotpot), and edomae-style sushi with pressed vinegared rice.

Kansai

Centered around Osaka and Kyoto. Famous for takoyaki (octopus balls), Osaka-style okonomiyaki, and Kyoto's refined kaiseki multi-course meals and yudofu (hot tofu).

Kyushu

Known for tonkotsu ramen with rich pork bone broth from Hakata, mentaiko (spicy cod roe), and Nagasaki champon noodle soup with seafood and vegetables.

Okinawa

Distinct island cuisine influenced by Chinese and Southeast Asian flavors. Specialties include goya champuru (bitter melon stir-fry), Okinawa soba, and rafute (stewed pork belly).

Japanese Dining Traditions

Kaiseki Izakaya Omakase Ryokan Meals Tea Ceremony Bentō

Kaiseki

Traditional multi-course Japanese dinner with precisely prepared dishes showcasing seasonal ingredients. Each small dish is artistically presented to enhance both taste and visual appeal.

Izakaya

Casual drinking establishments serving a variety of small, shareable dishes designed to accompany alcoholic beverages. A central part of Japan's after-work social culture.

Omakase

A chef's choice menu where diners leave their meal entirely up to the chef. Common in sushi restaurants, showcasing the chef's expertise and the freshest available ingredients.

Ryokan Meals

Elaborate meals served in traditional Japanese inns, typically featuring seasonal local specialties. Often served in guests' rooms or private dining areas on low tables.

Tea Ceremony

Ritualized preparation and presentation of matcha (powdered green tea), emphasizing mindfulness and respect. Often accompanied by small traditional sweets to balance the tea's bitterness.

Bentō

Single-portion takeout or home-packed meals in compartmentalized boxes. Traditional bento contains rice, protein, and various side dishes arranged aesthetically.

Welcome to Japan

Japan offers a captivating blend of ancient traditions and cutting-edge modernity. From tranquil temples and cherry blossoms to bustling cities and technological marvels, immerse yourself in a culture unlike any other.

Japan is an island country in East Asia, located in the northwest Pacific Ocean. It consists of four main islands: Honshu, Hokkaido, Kyushu, and Shikoku, along with thousands of smaller islands. With its unique culture, stunning natural landscapes, and blend of ancient traditions with futuristic technology, Japan continues to be one of the most fascinating destinations in the world.

Visit between November-February March-May for the best weather and cherry blossom season!

Japanese Cuisine

Sushi

A dish of vinegared rice combined with fresh raw fish, seafood, or vegetables. The rice is seasoned with rice vinegar, sugar, and salt, then topped or filled with ingredients like tuna, salmon, yellowtail, shrimp, or cucumber. Sushi has a delicate balance of flavors - slightly tart rice complementing the fresh fish, enhanced by small amounts of wasabi (spicy green paste), soy sauce, and pickled ginger.

Common types include:

  • Nigiri - hand-pressed rice topped with fish
  • Maki - rice and fillings rolled in seaweed
  • Sashimi - just the raw fish (technically not sushi)

Ramen

A noodle soup dish featuring wheat noodles served in a meat-based broth, flavored with soy sauce or miso, and topped with ingredients like sliced pork (chashu), dried seaweed (nori), green onions, and soft-boiled eggs.

The broth varieties include:

  1. Shoyu - soy sauce base
  2. Miso - fermented soybean paste base
  3. Shio - salt base
  4. Tonkotsu - pork bone base (simmered for hours)

The noodles range from thin and straight to thick and wavy, offering a complex, umami-rich taste experience.

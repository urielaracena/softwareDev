Tokyo & Surroundings

Experience the perfect blend of ultramodern and traditional in Japan's vibrant capital

Welcome to Tokyo

Tokyo is a city of fascinating contrasts‚Äîwhere ancient traditions blend seamlessly with cutting-edge technology and where serene gardens sit in the shadows of soaring skyscrapers. As Japan's bustling capital, Tokyo offers visitors an unforgettable journey through history, culture, cuisine, and innovation.

Tokyo Highlights

Shibuya Crossing

Experience the world's busiest pedestrian intersection where up to 3,000 people cross at once during peak times. The scramble crossing has become an iconic symbol of Tokyo's energy and is surrounded by shopping centers, restaurants, and entertainment venues.

Meiji Shrine

Find tranquility in the heart of Tokyo at this Shinto shrine dedicated to Emperor Meiji and Empress Shoken. Set within a 170-acre evergreen forest, the shrine offers a peaceful escape from the city's hustle with its towering torii gates and serene walking paths.

Akihabara Electric Town

Dive into Japan's otaku culture in this district famous for electronics, anime, manga and gaming. Browse multi-story electronics stores, visit themed caf√©s, explore colorful anime shops, or experience the excitement of Japanese arcade gaming centers.

Asakusa & Senso-ji Temple

Step back in time at Tokyo's oldest temple, dating back to 645 AD. Enter through the iconic Kaminarimon (Thunder Gate), stroll down the vibrant Nakamise shopping street lined with traditional crafts and snacks, and visit the impressive main hall.

Tokyo Skytree

Ascend 634 meters to the observation decks of the world's tallest tower for breathtaking panoramic views of Tokyo. During clear days, you might even spot Mount Fuji in the distance. The tower complex also features shopping malls and restaurants.

Harajuku & Takeshita Street

Explore the epicenter of Japan's youth fashion and culture. The lively Takeshita Street is packed with trendy boutiques, colorful crepe stands, and quirky specialty shops that showcase Japan's kawaii (cute) culture and avant-garde fashion trends.

Suggested 3-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Eastern Tokyo

  • Morning: Visit Asakusa district and Senso-ji Temple
  • Lunch: Try local street food at Nakamise Shopping Street
  • Afternoon: Explore the Tokyo National Museum in Ueno Park
  • Evening: Ascend Tokyo Skytree for night views and dinner

Day 2: Central Tokyo

  • Morning: Visit the Imperial Palace Gardens
  • Lunch: Taste fresh sushi at Tsukiji Outer Market
  • Afternoon: Experience tranquility at Meiji Shrine and explore Harajuku
  • Evening: Witness the energy of Shibuya Crossing and dine in the area

Day 3: Modern Tokyo

  • Morning: Visit teamLab Planets digital art museum
  • Lunch: Enjoy ramen at Tokyo Ramen Street in Tokyo Station
  • Afternoon: Explore electronics and anime culture in Akihabara
  • Evening: Experience nightlife in Shinjuku, with dinner at Memory Lane (Omoide Yokocho)

Day Trips from Tokyo

Hakone & Mount Fuji

Just 90 minutes from Tokyo, Hakone offers hot springs, lake cruises, and on clear days, spectacular views of Mount Fuji. Take the Hakone Round Course which includes a mountain railway, cable car, ropeway, and boat cruise on Lake Ashi.

Travel Time: 90 minutes by train

Nikko

Discover UNESCO World Heritage Sites including the lavishly decorated Toshogu Shrine, where Tokugawa Ieyasu, the founder of the Tokugawa shogunate, is enshrined. Nikko National Park also offers beautiful mountain scenery, waterfalls, and hot springs.

Travel Time: 2 hours by train

Kamakura

Often called "Little Kyoto," this coastal city is famous for the Great Buddha of Kamakura, a 13-meter-high bronze statue. Explore ancient temples, peaceful gardens, and the scenic Enoshima Island, connected to Kamakura by a bridge.

Travel Time: 1 hour by train

Kawagoe

Step back in time in "Little Edo," where Edo-period (1603-1867) merchant warehouses line the streets. Don't miss Kawagoe's Bell Tower, Candy Alley with traditional Japanese sweets, and the historic Kitain Temple which contains the only remaining buildings from the original Edo Castle.

Travel Time: 30 minutes by train
